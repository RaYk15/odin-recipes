Quiche Lorraine

Love me a salty pie, it has the best of both worlds. Super tasty, easy to eat and to ration, looks great... Incredible dish overall.
I would say this is the "hardest" recipe of the bunch I chose, but just because you have to prepare the dough mold first on the one hand, and then the filling on the other.
Even though, when you get to the cooking itself, just follow the recipe and you can't get it wrong.
Let's get to it!

INGREDIENTS

For the dough

  • 250g wheat flour
  • 125g butter
  • A pinch of salt
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 2 spoons of cold water

For the filling

  • 200g bacon
  • 500ml cooking cream
  • 3 eggs
  • 150g emmental cheese
  • Salt
  • White pepper
  • Grounded nutmeg

UTENSILS

  • Mixing bowl
  • Round mold
  • Rolling pin
  • Oven paper

PREPARATION

For the dough

  1. Add the flour and the butter (diced) in the mixing bowl with the pinched salt.
  2. Mix it with your fingers until it gets a sandy texture.
  3. Add the egg yolk and the water and mix without working the dough too much. Until the dough doesn't stick to the bowl.
  4. Wrap it in film and let it rest in the refrigerator for 30 mins.
  5. Take it out, stretch with the rolling pin and put the dough over the mold (previously oiled with butter and flour).
  6. Put the oven paper over the dough, and put some weight on it so it doesn't rise (i.e. chickpeas or some other legume you have at home).
  7. Bake in the oven for 10 mins at 180 degrees. Heat over and under.

For the filling

  1. Dice the bacon.
  2. Fry the bacon in a pan.
  3. Mix in the bowl the cooking cream and the eggs.
  4. Grate the emmental cheese and mix it in the bowl.
  5. Season the mix with salt, white pepper, and nutmeg.
  6. Take the dough out of the oven, take off the weight, and pour the mix into the dough.
  7. Finally, bake it in the oven for 30 mins at 180 degrees with heat over and under.

That's it, now you wait for the quiche to cool down and serve.
